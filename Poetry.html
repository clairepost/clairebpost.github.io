a spanish love letter to Kyle


Esto es mi amigo Kyle.  

I only really became friends with him this summer during the coronavirus pandemic and the Friends were so scarce to find.  This day was the first time we rock climbed together, the first time I ever rock climbed in Arkansas, which feels like such a strange thing in retrospect having climbed so much now.  He was so confident here, confident I would not let him fall, that I would remember what he told me about the belay and how to give slack as he clipped in.  I was so rusty, and even though he was just doing an easy route, he still believed in me enough to let me belay him.  When you rock climb you literally put your life in the hands of another, a rope and their hands are the only things separating you from death.  

You can see it in the picture here.  The blue one and the yellow metal of the clips on his belt.  If you got really close to the rocks, you. Could. See the metal studs sticking out and the fine layer of dust that covers every crevice in the rocks.  If you could hear in the picture you would know there is the Lincoln Lake behind us and the occasional fishermen and fisherwomen paddling along in flat kayaks along it.  You could also see the trees and hear that dry wáter sound they make as they brush the sky.  You would feel the heat and the screaming melody of cicadas in the 100 degree Arkansan heat.  Kyle sweats a bit in the picture because it is so hot and his legs are dirty from the grime on the rocks that will not get off because it is so humid in July.  If you  were in this moment in time you might also hear his voice laughing at me, making jokes about letting him fall, and the sweet jazz music playing from his speaker.  All in the middle of the grass and trees and growing things.

Sometimes while we climbed bugs would crawl on us: butterflies and moths, horse flies and spiders, knats and wasps.  No matter what it was you had to be calm.  Kyle is not torn up any in the. Picture because he was such a good climber he hardly ever fell.  He never lost his calm.  He really made a great teacher and helped me find something new to love in a summer when so many things seemed to be taken away
